Title: The Quiet Rise of Small Language Models
Author: Jordan Rivera

For years the story of artificial intelligence has been a story of scale:
bigger models, more parameters, larger training runs. But a quieter trend is
reshaping how teams actually ship AI features. Small, efficient language models
now handle a surprising share of real-world tasks — summarizing tickets,
routing emails, extracting fields from documents — at a fraction of the cost
and latency of their giant cousins.

The appeal is practical. A model that runs cheaply can be called millions of
times a day without wrecking a budget. It can run closer to the user, cutting
round-trip delays. And for narrow, well-defined jobs, a smaller model that has
been given clear instructions often performs as well as a much larger one.

None of this means large models are going away. Frontier systems still set the
ceiling on what is possible, and teams reach for them when a task genuinely
demands deep reasoning. But the day-to-day plumbing of AI software is
increasingly built from small, fast, unglamorous models doing focused work
reliably. The future of applied AI, it turns out, may be less about size and
more about fit.
